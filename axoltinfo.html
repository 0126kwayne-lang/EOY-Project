Axolotls are critically endangered, fully aquatic salamanders native only to Lake Xochimilco near Mexico City. Known for neoteny, they retain juvenile traits like external gills and live 10–15 years, growing up to 12 inches long. They are famous for regenerating lost limbs and organs, and they eat a carnivorous diet of worms, insects, and small fish. Neoteny (Juvenile Features): Unlike other salamanders, they rarely undergo metamorphosis to live on land, keeping their gills and aquatic lifestyle throughout their lives. Reproduction: Females can lay hundreds of eggs, often 200 to over 1,000 at a time, which hatch in 2-3 weeks.
