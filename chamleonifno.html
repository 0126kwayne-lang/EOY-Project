Chameleons are specialized, mostly arboreal lizards known for their ability to change skin color for communication and temperature regulation, not just camouflage. They possess unique adaptations, including independently moving eyes, projectile sticky tongues often longer than their bodies, and specialized, gripping feet. Color Change: Used for regulating body temperature (darker to absorb heat, lighter to reflect) and communicating mood or signaling to rivals/mates. Diet: Primarily insectivores (crickets, locusts), though larger species may eat small birds. Habit: Most are arboreal (tree-dwelling) and possess a prehensile tail for stability. Vision: Each eye can rotate independently (180 horizontal,90vertical), providing 360 vision, though they lock both eyes together for depth perception when hunting.
