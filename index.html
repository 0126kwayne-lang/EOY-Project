Amphibian and Reptiles

Reptiles are air-breathing, ectothermic (cold-blooded) vertebrates covered in scales or scaly skin, encompassing over 12,000 species. Found worldwide in various habitats, major groups include snakes, lizards, turtles, and crocodilians. The largest living reptile is the saltwater crocodile, while some dwarf geckos are among the smallest. Unlike amphibians, reptiles evolved a shelled, water-retaining egg that allows them to reproduce in dry environments without needing a water source for larval stages. They first appeared over 315 million years ago during the Carboniferous period.Genetic Ties to Birds: While we often group them by appearance, crocodilians are genetically more closely related to birds than they are to lizards or snakes.

Amphibian are Amphibians are cold-blooded (ectothermic) vertebrates that live "double lives"—typically starting as aquatic, gill-breathing larvae and metamorphosing into air-breathing adults that live on land, though many stay near water. Key characteristics include moist, permeable skin used for respiration, four limbs (tetrapods), and a reliance on moist habitats. Egg-Laying (Anamniotic): Unlike birds or reptiles, most amphibians lay jelly-like eggs that lack a hard shell, requiring them to be deposited in water or very damp places to prevent drying out. Unique Heart Structure: They possess a three-chambered heart (two atria and one ventricle), which is an evolutionary middle ground between the two-chambered hearts of fish and the four-chambered hearts of mammals. The Three Orders: It's helpful to note the three distinct types: Anura (frogs and toads), Caudata (salamanders and newts), and Gymnophiona (caecilians—limbless, serpentine amphibians). Environmental Indicators: Because their skin is so permeable, they absorb toxins easily.This makes them "bioindicators," meaning their health (or decline) reflects the overall health of their ecosystem.

Amphibians and reptiles are both cold-blooded vertebrates, but differ fundamentally in skin, reproduction, and lifecycle. Amphibians (frogs, salamanders) have moist, permeable skin, lay gelatinous eggs in water, and undergo metamorphosis. Reptiles (snakes, lizards, turtles) have dry, scaly skin, lay leathery, amniotic eggs on land, and breathe with lungs.
